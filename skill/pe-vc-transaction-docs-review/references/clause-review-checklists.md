# Clause Review Checklists

Review every relevant clause below for both RMB and offshore structures. The structure changes the legal mechanics; it does not remove the clause from review.

For each family, record `reviewed`, `not applicable`, `missing document`, or
`deferred`. Do not treat absence from the issue log as evidence that the family
was reviewed.

## 0. Definitions, Priority, and Package Integrity

- Parties, group companies, founders, major investors, affiliates, competitors, and control.
- Qualified IPO/QIPO, liquidation event, redemption event, material breach, material adverse effect, and business day.
- Capitalization table, share/registered-capital numbers, percentages, conversion prices, and pre/post-money arithmetic.
- Document hierarchy among investment/subscription agreement, SHA/IRA, Articles/章程, disclosure letter, side letters, resolutions, and schedules.
- Entire-agreement, amendment, waiver, conflict, accession/deed of adherence, and rights-survival mechanics.
- Missing schedules, blanks, inconsistent defined terms, broken cross-references, signature blocks, and execution authority.

## 1. Economics and Closing

- Investment amount, valuation, pre/post-money, price per share/registered capital.
- Primary vs secondary sale allocation.
- Payment timing, currency, account, taxes, capital reserve.
- For RMB limited liability companies: subscribed vs paid-in registered capital, contribution deadlines, statutory/transition-period compliance, acceleration, contribution-default remedies, shareholder forfeiture, and whether articles mirror the agreed mechanics.
- For founder secondary sales in RMB limited liability companies: whether the transferred registered capital has been fully paid, tax allocation, old-share pricing, and whether the buyer or seller bears unpaid-capital statutory risk.
- Conditions precedent, closing deliverables, approvals, bring-down certificate.
- Long-stop date, termination, consequences of failed closing.
- For M&A or mixed primary/secondary transactions: locked-box, completion accounts,
  earn-out or hybrid price mechanics; cash/debt/working-capital definitions;
  leakage, accounting hierarchy, calculation review and expert determination.
- For cross-border funding: funds certainty, account path, approval/filing,
  foreign-exchange registration, use-of-proceeds restrictions, failed-funding
  allocation and whether closing steps reflect current rather than superseded procedures.
- Investor profile: state-owned/government-backed investor, listed-company investor, strategic investor, financial investor, or competitor-sensitive investor. Check internal approval, signing authority, investment restrictions, information firewall, and cooperation agreement linkage where relevant.

## 2. Representations, Warranties, Disclosure, Indemnity

- Warrantor identity: company, founders, subsidiaries, WFOE/VIE entities.
- Fundamental vs business warranties.
- Disclosure letter scope: specific vs general disclosure; data room disclosure.
- Knowledge group, actual/deemed knowledge, materiality scrape, update duty,
  bring-down standard, anti-sandbagging/sandbagging and non-reliance language.
- Survival, limitation period, cap, basket/deductible, de minimis, fraud carve-out.
- Indemnity obligors, joint/several liability, founder cap, collection sequence.
- Contingent liabilities, specific indemnities, causation, mitigation, insurance,
  third-party recovery, tax treatment, exclusive remedy and double-recovery controls.

## 3. Preemptive Right

- Scope: new shares, registered capital, securities, convertible instruments.
- Holder: all investors, major investors, specific investors.
- Allocation: absolute/pro rata vs relative among exercising holders.
- Over-allotment.
- Exceptions: ESOP, strategic issuances, M&A consideration, approved issuances.

## 4. ROFR and Co-Sale

- Restricted transferors: founders/common shareholders, all shareholders, investors.
- ROFR holders and order: company first, investors, other shareholders.
- Allocation and over-purchase.
- Co-sale formula and whether control-change triggers full tag.
- Permitted transfers and family/trust/affiliate carve-outs.

## 5. Redemption

- Triggers: QIPO failure, material breach, founder misconduct, regulatory illegality, VIE invalidity, license/IP failure, other investor redemption.
- Cure period for breach.
- Obligors: company, founders, second-ranking founders, guarantors.
- Price formula and interest/multiple.
- Exercise mechanics: trigger confirmation, opening date, contractual exercise
  window, notice content and service, waiver/reservation, partial exercise and
  limitation-period linkage. Do not present the official six-month reference
  view as a statutory hard limit.
- Price mechanics: principal, contractual return, compounding, taxes, post-due
  interest/liquidated damages, payment allocation, set-off, currency and double-recovery risk.
- Performance: payment schedule, capital reduction/share transfer mechanics,
  partial redemption, approvals, creditor process, registration and alternative path.
- Caps and founder liability limits.
- Transfer/release: assumption, obligee consent, novation, continuing liability,
  founder departure, spouse/guarantee scope and security realization.
- Insolvency and enforcement: acceleration, claim filing, preservation, asset
  location, service and cross-border recognition where relevant.
- Current market context: 2025 adoption declined, especially offshore; do not assume redemption is near-universal. If present, focus on collectability, company-only risk, and founder liability boundaries.

## 6. Anti-Dilution

- Formula: broad-based weighted average, narrow-based weighted average, full ratchet.
- Excluded issuances: ESOP, conversion, strategic, M&A, approved issuance, debt conversion.
- RMB implementation: nominal capital increase, founder transfer, cash compensation.
- Offshore implementation: conversion price adjustment and Articles consistency.

## 7. Liquidation Preference

- Liquidation events: liquidation, dissolution, merger, sale of all/substantially all assets, change of control.
- Seniority among rounds.
- Participating vs non-participating; capped participating.
- Preference amount: multiple, investment amount plus interest, higher-of formula.
- Interaction with redemption and drag/sale proceeds.
- Current market context: participating preference remains common, but 2025 data supports company-side fallbacks to non-participating or capped participating, especially in offshore structures.

## 8. Drag-Along

- Drag holders and approval thresholds.
- Trigger conditions: time, valuation, shareholder approval, board approval.
- Founder consent or founder director consent.
- Treatment of warranties, indemnity, non-compete, escrow, rollover.
- Whether dragged shareholders must bear obligations beyond ownership warranties.

## 9. Protective Provisions and Board

- Reserved matters list: equity, debt, budget, related-party transactions, acquisitions, liquidation, IPO, ESOP, hiring/firing, business scope.
- Approval level: shareholder class, major investor, investor director, board.
- Thresholds and dilution cut-off.
- Deadlock and emergency exceptions.
- Board/observer rights, confidentiality, committees, quorum.
- Director appointment threshold, actual appointment at closing, director indemnity agreement, D&O insurance, and local-law fiduciary/local-counsel issues.
- Founder veto requests where founder post-closing holding falls near or below control thresholds.
- Whether veto, board, quorum, information or negative-control rights create
  merger-control, consolidation, foreign-investment review or regulated-investor consequences.

## 10. Founder Restrictions

- Full-time commitment, non-compete, non-solicit, confidentiality.
- Transfer restriction and permitted transfers.
- Restricted equity vesting/release schedule.
- Good leaver / bad leaver / cause definitions.
- Repurchase/purchase price and buyer.
- Founder removal, resignation, handover, IP return/assignment, system access,
  continuing cooperation and whether departure changes any redemption obligation.

## 11. ESOP

- Pool size, pre-money vs post-money treatment.
- Approved plan vs reserved pool.
- Holder platform for RMB deals; option pool for offshore deals.
- Approval rights for grants, amendments, acceleration, repricing.
- Grant authority, vesting start, cliff, performance conditions, change-of-control
  acceleration, good/bad leaver, repurchase buyer/price and cap-table reconciliation.
- RMB plans: check whether incentives are options, restricted equity, or both; whether grants are through a limited partnership holding platform; capital-increase vs founder-transfer path for the platform; tax, registration, and 50-shareholder-limit issues.
- Offshore plans: check option pool reserve in authorized share capital, direct issuance mechanics, PRC employee SAFE/foreign-exchange registration issues, and Articles consistency.

## 12. Information, Inspection, and Management Rights

- Monthly/quarterly/annual financials, budget, audit, KPI reporting.
- Inspection scope, notice, frequency, confidentiality, non-disruption.
- Threshold for major investors.
- Strategic investor information firewall.
- For listed-company, strategic, or competitor-sensitive investors, check whether information rights trigger disclosure, MNPI, antitrust, trade secret, or business-separation concerns.

## 13. Investor Transfer Restrictions and MFN

- Affiliate/fund transfers.
- Competitor restrictions and company consent.
- Transfer of rights with shares.
- MFN scope: same round, existing investors, side letters, future financing.
- MFN exclusions: senior economic rights, regulatory rights, strategic cooperation.
- Side-letter authority, amendment/waiver thresholds, document priority, disclosure
  to other investors, partial-investor settlements and no-prejudice mechanics.
- Absolute no-transfer restrictions to company competitors or strategic-investor competitors.
- Restrictions on investor investing in or cooperating with specified third parties, and loss of investor rights if breached.
- 2025 MFN frequency declined materially; do not assume future-financing MFN is a default investor right.

## 14. Valuation Adjustment / Gamble

- Performance target, compensation trigger, obligor, compensation method.
- Relationship with redemption.
- Enforceability, capital reduction, tax, accounting, and dispute risk.

## 15. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

- Governing law by document and structure.
- Arbitration institution, seat, language, number of arbitrators.
- Interim measures, preservation, emergency arbitrator, service.
- Consistency across SPA/SHA/Articles/side letters.
- Joinder, consolidation, parallel proceedings, nonsignatory obligors/guarantors,
  survival after termination or invalidity, and conflicts caused by transfer or novation.
- Local counsel confirmation for non-PRC law.
- For PRC-law arbitration after 2026-03-01, check the current Arbitration Law,
  including selected institution, scope, seat for foreign-related arbitration,
  interim measures, service, and applicable institutional rules.

## 16. Regulatory, Compliance, Data, and Sector Conditions

- Foreign investment restrictions, negative-list issues, ODI/FDI/SAFE and foreign-exchange steps where relevant.
- For foreign investment, check investor nationality/control, target sector,
  current negative-list restrictions, national treatment, security-review
  filing, PRC natural-person participation, and SAFE Circular 37/round-trip
  registration facts rather than inferring compliance from the structure label.
- Antitrust/merger-control, national-security review, state-asset approval/valuation, and listed-company disclosure where triggered. For merger control, separately determine control, turnover thresholds, filing timing and gun-jumping risk.
- For public property-exchange state-asset transactions, compare the agreement
  with the announcement, selection result and 2025 operation rules, including
  applicable buyback, benefit-compensation, nominee-holding, disguised-debt and
  price-adjustment restrictions. Do not apply those restrictions merely because
  one investor is state-owned.
- For insurance capital, determine control/joint control and look-through status,
  regulatory approval, own-funds and industry limits, prohibited lending/guarantee
  support, cross-holding, control layers and anti-avoidance constraints.
- For PRC outbound investments from 2026-07-01, map approval/filing, information
  reporting, cross-border funds registration, outbound security review, export
  control, data, merger-control, tax and state-asset conditions.
- Anti-bribery, sanctions/export control, AML/KYC, related-party transactions, and beneficial-owner representations where material.
- Cybersecurity, data export, personal information, source-code/model/data access, and investor information-firewall restrictions for data-sensitive businesses.
- For data-heavy matters, identify personal/sensitive personal information,
  important data, processor scale, data-room/inspection access, overseas
  recipients and the applicable security-assessment, standard-contract,
  certification or exemption route under the current rules.
- Sector licenses, IP ownership, open-source/AI training-data exposure, key-person employment, and change-of-control consent requirements.
- For AI/robotics targets, also test model and training-data rights, employee and
  contractor IP chain, algorithms/filings, product and safety exposure, hardware
  and supply-chain rights, source-code access and cybersecurity.
- IPO/listing termination, suspension, restoration, or conversion of special shareholder rights.

## 17. Tax, Costs, Confidentiality, and Publicity

- Withholding, stamp duty, founder secondary-sale tax, capital reduction tax, gross-up, and tax cooperation.
- Cross-border structure, permanent-establishment, beneficial-owner, indirect
  transfer, profit repatriation, tax covenant, audit control and tax-indemnity allocation.
- Transaction expenses, legal fees, failed-closing costs, and expense caps.
- Confidentiality scope, permitted disclosures, professional advisers, regulators, LPs, publicity, and announcement consent.
- Strategic-investor cooperation obligations, exclusivity/no-shop, non-disparagement, and information firewalls.

## 18. Notices and Other Boilerplate

- Notice method, address, deemed receipt, electronic delivery, and service for arbitration/litigation.
- Assignment, successor liability, affiliate transfers, third-party rights, severability, counterparts, e-signatures, and language priority.
- Express assumption, obligee consent, release/novation and continuing-liability
  mechanics for obligations intended to move with equity or survive a transfer.
- Further assurances, specific performance, cumulative remedies, no waiver, force majeure, and time-is-of-the-essence provisions.
