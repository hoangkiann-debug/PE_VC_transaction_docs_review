# Negotiation Pattern Stress Tests

Use these prompts after the base clause checklist and the current-practice stress
tests. They supplement the substantive framework with version-chain and drafting
mechanics. They do not replace the benchmark data, legal authority protocol,
structure playbooks, or party-side positions.

## Evidence Boundary

- A historical draft shows that wording moved; it does not prove which party
  proposed it, why it moved, whether the outcome was optimal, or whether it is
  current market practice.
- A signed or final version is an outcome for one fact pattern, not a model clause.
- Do not infer client side from an insertion or deletion alone. When the side is
  unknown, describe the economic, control, execution and enforcement effect
  neutrally, then apply the current matter's confirmed party position.
- Never use historical drafting as current legal authority. Re-check current law,
  transition rules, authority status and governing-law limitations under
  `legal-authority-protocol.md`.
- Do not include names, file paths, revision authors, matter identifiers or
  traceable extracts from another matter in any work product.

## Version-Chain Method

For a later-round or precedent-informed review:

1. Identify the current draft, baseline, redline/clean relationship and apparent
   round. Do not use a final version as proof of the route taken to reach it.
2. Classify each material movement by its practical control: holder, obligor,
   trigger, threshold, time, notice, consent, waiver, price/formula, liability,
   remedy, implementation and cross-document effect.
3. Separate textual movement from legal or commercial judgment. Added language
   is not automatically investor-favorable; deleted language is not automatically
   company-favorable.
4. Test the movement across the full package, especially definitions, cap table,
   articles, approvals, disclosure, side letters and dispute clauses.
5. Convert the result into preferred, balanced and fallback positions only after
   the current client side and facts are known.

## High-Movement Drafting Controls

### Definitions and thresholds

- Treat a changed definition as a package-level change. Trace every use before
  accepting a revised affiliate, control, investor-majority, qualified IPO,
  material breach, competitor, cause, bad-leaver or permitted-transfer concept.
- For every holder threshold, state numerator, denominator, measurement time,
  dilution treatment, affiliate aggregation, class/series treatment and whether
  the threshold is tested before or after the relevant transfer or issuance.
- Check whether a threshold unintentionally switches off board, information,
  veto, transfer, pre-emption or exit rights after dilution.

### Governance and board mechanics

- Review appointment, removal and replacement together with board size, quorum,
  voting threshold, casting vote, observer rights, committees and deadlock.
- Distinguish an investor consent right from a board vote. Identify the holder,
  voting group, response period, deemed consent or refusal, emergency exception
  and consequences of acting without approval.
- For reserved matters, test materiality and monetary thresholds, ordinary-course
  carve-outs, approved-budget exceptions and whether multiple provisions regulate
  the same act inconsistently.

### Capitalization and contribution

- Reconcile registered capital, paid-in capital, capital reserve, investment
  amount, share count, price per share, ownership percentages and timing across
  the investment agreement, shareholders agreement, articles, cap table and
  approvals.
- For contribution deadlines and defaults, identify the payer, amount, account,
  date, evidence, cure route, statutory process and consequences for ownership,
  voting, distributions, forfeiture, transfer and further contribution.
- Recalculate percentages and dilution whenever a draft changes an amount,
  option pool, conversion, secondary transfer or capitalization assumption.

### Redemption

- Treat holder, obligor and liability form as separate negotiation controls.
  Check company, founder, controller, guarantor and transferee obligations;
  several versus joint liability; ordering; cap; security; and release/novation.
- Test each trigger for materiality, fault, knowledge, causation, cure, evidence,
  cross-trigger and objective ascertainability.
- Test exercise notice, opening date, contractual window, waiver, partial
  exercise and limitation-period interaction separately from the trigger.
- Recalculate price, simple/compound return, tax, post-due remedies, payment
  allocation and double-recovery risk. Confirm an implementable company, founder
  or transfer route under current law.

### Transfer-right sequence

- Build one sequence for permitted transfer, investor transfer, ROFR, pre-emption,
  co-sale, drag, consent and competitor restrictions. Do not review each clause
  in isolation.
- For every step, identify notice content, recipient, response period, waiver,
  allocation, oversubscription, same-terms requirement, closing deadline and the
  consequence of a failed or changed third-party sale.
- For drag and co-sale, test initiating-holder thresholds, minority protection,
  consideration form, representations, liability caps, escrow, allocation and
  whether holders can be forced to give business warranties or joint liability.

### Closing and waiver

- Allocate responsibility for every condition precedent and closing deliverable.
  Identify who determines satisfaction, who may waive, whether waiver is partial,
  and whether the same item must be repeated or brought down at closing.
- Reconcile long-stop date, extension control, regulatory delay, cure, termination,
  deposits, costs, specific performance and survival.
- Distinguish a true closing condition from a covenant, deliverable, post-closing
  undertaking or remedy. Avoid conditions that depend entirely on the beneficiary's
  subjective satisfaction without an agreed standard.

### Economic rights

- For liquidation preference, model seniority, participation, cap, conversion,
  deemed liquidation, non-cash consideration, escrow/holdback and allocation
  across series. Check interaction with redemption and dividends.
- For anti-dilution, verify the formula, broad/narrow denominator, excluded
  issuances, option-pool changes, pay-to-play, notice, waiver and implementation
  in the articles and cap table.
- A wording refinement can change the formula without changing the clause title.
  Recalculate rather than relying on labels such as weighted average or full ratchet.

### Representations, indemnity and disclosure

- Read materiality, knowledge, fault, causation, disclosure and bring-down
  qualifiers together; check whether they are duplicated, sandbagged, removed by
  a specific indemnity or overridden by an exclusive-remedy clause.
- For indemnity, map claimant, obligor, loss definition, causation, mitigation,
  third-party claims, tax/insurance recovery, basket, cap, survival, fraud carve-out,
  procedure, control of defence and payment timing.
- Do not treat a general data-room disclosure as equivalent to fair, specific or
  deemed disclosure without confirming the agreed standard.

### ESOP and founder arrangements

- State whether the pool is measured pre- or post-money, who bears dilution,
  whether ungranted awards are included, and who approves grants and amendments.
- Reconcile vesting, acceleration, good/bad leaver, repurchase price, exercise,
  tax, service, IP assignment, restrictive covenants and founder transfer rules.
- Check that founder liability, share-based caps and leaver consequences use a
  defined valuation date and workable transfer/payment mechanism.

### Dispute, priority and survival

- Compare governing law, forum, seat, scope, service, interim measures, joinder
  and consolidation across every operative document and undertaking.
- State which document prevails for each conflict and whether the priority clause
  binds all relevant parties. A general priority clause does not repair a missing
  signatory, corporate approval or implementation document.
- Check survival after termination, rescission, transfer, amendment and novation
  for confidentiality, indemnity, redemption, dispute and accrued rights.

## Output Use

Use these stress tests to improve issue spotting and fallback design. Do not cite
them as frequency evidence. Market statements still require the benchmark layer,
and every current legal conclusion still requires the authority-status gate.
