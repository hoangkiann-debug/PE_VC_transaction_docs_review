# Recent Practice Stress Tests (2024-2026)

Use these prompts after the base clause checklist. They distill recurring failure
patterns identified in recent professional commentary and dispute analysis. They
are issue-spotting prompts, not legal authority or market-frequency evidence.

For a legal conclusion, route the issue through `legal-authority-protocol.md` and
the current authority registry. For a market-practice statement, use the annual
benchmark data instead. Do not identify commentary sources in ordinary client
outputs.

## Redemption Lifecycle

Review redemption as a sequence, not one clause:

1. Grant: holder, obligor, priority among obligors, several or joint liability,
   security, guarantee, and whether the obligation survives a transfer or exit.
2. Trigger: objective event, knowledge/fault qualifier, materiality, cure,
   causation, evidence source, cross-trigger, and who confirms occurrence.
3. Exercise: opening date, contractual exercise window, notice content, delivery,
   irrevocability, partial exercise, waiver, reservation of rights, and interaction
   with limitation periods. The bundled Supreme People's Court reference answer
   is nonbinding guidance; never state that six months is a statutory hard limit.
4. Price: principal, contractual return, compounding, taxes, withholding,
   post-due interest or liquidated damages, payment-order allocation, valuation
   date, set-off, currency, and partial-payment mechanics. Check for double
   recovery and adjustment risk rather than assuming every agreed component will
   be awarded cumulatively.
5. Performance: capital reduction, share transfer, payment schedule, approvals,
   creditor process, registration, solvency/capital maintenance, cooperation, and
   an implementable alternative if company performance is unavailable.
6. Enforcement: forum, preservation, service, asset location, insolvency,
   acceleration, claim filing, security realization, and cross-border recognition.

Additional stress tests:

- A founder's share transfer, resignation, or departure does not by itself answer
  whether redemption liability was transferred or released. Require express
  assumption, obligee consent, release/novation conditions, and continuing
  liability where intended.
- For a founder cap based on shares or share value, define the valuation date,
  valuation method, negative-value treatment, transfer restrictions, interim
  leakage, security and fault/fraud carve-outs.
- If spouse consent, guarantee, or marital-property recourse matters, identify the
  exact signatory, capacity, consent language, scope and local enforceability.
  Do not infer spouse liability from marital status.
- Test whether a redemption or valuation-adjustment side letter signed with only
  some investors conflicts with amendment thresholds, equal treatment, MFN,
  document priority, corporate authority or other investors' rights.

## Multi-Document Dispute Resolution

- Compare governing law, institution, seat/place, scope, language, arbitrator
  number, service and interim-measure language across every agreement, articles,
  guarantee, side letter and deed of adherence.
- Determine whether related claims can be joined or consolidated and whether
  inconsistent clauses create parallel proceedings or jurisdiction challenges.
- Check clause survival after termination, invalidity, rescission, transfer and
  novation; check whether nonsignatory founders, guarantors or affiliates are
  actually bound.
- For PRC-law arbitration, use the Arbitration Law effective from 2026-03-01 and
  the selected institution's current rules. For any non-PRC seat or governing
  law, mark local-counsel questions separately.

## Regulated and Cross-Border Investors

Classify the investor and transaction before applying a regulatory checklist:

- State-owned asset transaction: determine the ownership chain, transaction
  type, approval route, whether the deal is publicly conducted through a property
  exchange, appraisal/filing status, announcement and selection terms, and which
  party controls each approval. For an in-scope public transfer or capital
  increase, compare the agreement against the 2025 operation rules, including
  restrictions on buyback, benefit compensation, nominee holding, disguised debt
  and post-selection price adjustment. Do not extend those restrictions to every
  state-owned investor without an applicability analysis.
- Insurance capital: determine whether the investment and related holdings create
  control or joint control, whether look-through or aggregation applies, whether
  regulatory approval is a condition, and whether own-funds, industry, lending,
  guarantee, cross-holding and control-layer restrictions affect the documents.
- Foreign or outbound investment: identify investor nationality/residency,
  direct and indirect control, target business, funding path, ODI/FDI/SAFE facts,
  legacy defects and the party responsible for remediation.
- For PRC outbound investments after 2026-07-01, map approval/filing, information
  reporting, cross-border funds registration, security review, export control,
  data, merger control, tax and state-asset steps. Allocate reasonable-efforts,
  information, remedy, long-stop and termination consequences without promising
  an unlawful closing.
- Apply the 2025 cross-border investment/financing FX reforms to the actual bank
  account and funds flow. Do not keep a deleted registration step in the closing
  checklist, but do not treat procedural simplification as a waiver of authenticity,
  use-of-proceeds, access or other foreign-exchange requirements.

## Capital and Governance Mechanics

- Capital reserve, paid-in capital and registered capital are different concepts.
  If a transaction converts capital reserve or another account into capital,
  confirm accounting source, shareholder entitlement, resolutions, statutory
  procedure, registration and cap-table consequences.
- For selective capital reduction, distinguish corporate approval, contractual
  consent, creditor protection, equal-treatment issues and the payment path.
  Do not infer a universally sufficient voting threshold from a single case.
- For shareholder forfeiture, align the contractual default remedy with the
  statutory demand, minimum grace period, board action, notice, disposal/cancellation
  and any follow-on contribution obligation.
- Check every transfer restriction, veto, board right, information right and
  leaver mechanism against the articles, resolutions, capitalization table and
  regulatory-control analysis.

## M&A Drafting Modules

Price adjustment:

- Identify locked-box, completion accounts, earn-out or hybrid mechanics.
- Define cash, debt, working capital, leakage, permitted leakage, accounting
  principles, hierarchy, consistency, information access, review periods,
  dispute notice, expert determination, finality and payment timing.
- Separate price adjustment from damages and indemnity, and prevent double recovery.

Representations, disclosure and indemnity:

- Connect each warranty to disclosure standard, knowledge group, materiality,
  update duty, bring-down, survival, remedy, cap, basket and fraud carve-out.
- For contingent liabilities, decide whether general data-room disclosure is
  sufficient, whether a specific indemnity is needed, and how causation, tax,
  insurance, third-party recovery and mitigation affect recovery.
- Test non-reliance, exclusive-remedy and entire-agreement clauses against the
  intended fraud, concealment and pre-contractual statement treatment.

Signing-to-closing period:

- Define ordinary course, consent items, emergency exceptions, budget deviations,
  leakage, new debt, hiring, contracts, litigation, distributions and transaction
  access rights.
- Avoid giving the buyer or investor pre-closing operational control that creates
  merger-control, gun-jumping, fiduciary, confidentiality or business-continuity
  risk. Use clean teams or bounded consultation where needed.
- Allocate interim economic benefit/risk, material change, breach notification,
  cure, termination and specific performance coherently.

## ESOP, Founder and Sector Diligence

- ESOP: reconcile pool size, pre/post-money treatment, holder/platform, grant
  authority, vesting, acceleration, good/bad leaver, repurchase price, tax,
  registration, foreign-exchange and articles/cap-table implementation.
- Founder/team: verify service status, IP assignment, prior employer restrictions,
  continuous entrepreneurship conflicts, spouse/nominee holdings, key-person
  dependency, removal and handover mechanics.
- AI and robotics: verify IP and model ownership, employee/contractor assignments,
  training-data rights, open-source use, licenses/filings, algorithm/model or
  product compliance, hardware/supply-chain rights, safety and cybersecurity.
- Data-heavy targets: classify personal/sensitive personal information and
  important data, scope data-room access, overseas adviser/investor access,
  privacy audit status, incidents, processor agreements and remediation.
- Regulated assets or sectors: convert title, license, subsidy, permit, change-of-
  control and renewal risks into specific warranties, conditions, covenants,
  indemnities and objective exit triggers rather than a generic compliance clause.

## Scope Boundary

Do not use this reference to expand the skill silently. Private-fund formation,
fund operations, bankruptcy restructuring investment agreements, already-listed
target valuation-adjustment or securities disputes, and biotech licensing/BD
agreements remain outside scope unless the user expressly requests and authorizes
that separate document family. Pre-IPO special-rights treatment for an unlisted
target remains in scope, as does a listed-company investor investing in an
unlisted target.
