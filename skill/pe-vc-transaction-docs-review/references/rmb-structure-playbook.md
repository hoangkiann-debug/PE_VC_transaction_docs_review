# RMB Onshore Structure Playbook

Use when the PRC entity is the financing subject and investors directly hold equity/registered capital in the PRC company.

## 2025 Market Signals

- RMB/onshore structures remained the majority but fell to 66.51% of 2025 projects; use the 2024/2025 average of 70.05% when a smoothed benchmark is needed.
- Pure domestic structures reached 73.05% of onshore projects in 2025, while foreign-invested onshore structures were 26.95%.
- 87.20% of 2025 onshore financing subjects were limited liability companies.
- State-owned/government-backed investors appeared in over 50% of onshore projects. When present, check internal approvals, investment scope, signing authority, valuation/compliance process, and any special reporting or exit constraints.
- In 2025 onshore LLC projects, founders had not fully paid registered capital before closing in 62.39% of cases, mostly because the contribution deadline had not arrived.

## Core Review Focus

Every key VC/PE clause in the benchmark remains relevant under RMB structure:

- Investment amount, valuation, registered capital increase, capital contribution deadlines, payment mechanics.
- Conditions precedent, closing deliverables, approvals, shareholder resolutions, amended articles.
- Representations, warranties, disclosure, survival, indemnity.
- Preemptive right, ROFR, co-sale, drag-along.
- Redemption, valuation adjustment, anti-dilution, liquidation preference, preferred dividend.
- Protective provisions, board seats, information and inspection rights.
- Founder restrictions, restricted equity, leaver repurchase/purchase, founder secondary sales.
- Investor transfer restrictions, competitor restrictions, MFN, side letters.
- Governing law, arbitration, preservation measures, service, enforcement.

## RMB-Specific Legal/Execution Checks

Company redemption and capital reduction:

- A PRC limited liability company redemption often requires capital reduction, share transfer, or another implementable structure.
- Check whether company redemption conflicts with capital maintenance, creditor notice, shareholder approval, articles, and registration mechanics.
- Check Company Law Articles 224-226 for the current reduction procedure and
  consequences of unlawful reduction; do not cite market practice as proof that
  a company redemption path is legally performable.
- If company redemption is the primary remedy, investor-side review should consider founder joint/several, second-ranking, guarantee-like, or equity-value cap mechanics.
- Company/founder-side review should seek trigger specificity, cure periods, second-ranking liability, caps, and no acceleration for immaterial breaches.
- Separate trigger, exercise window/notice, price and post-due remedies,
  implementation and enforcement. If no exercise window is stated, flag the
  nonbinding official reference view on a reasonable period rather than calling
  six months a statutory deadline.
- For selective capital reduction, identify statutory approval and creditor
  steps, contractual consent, equality issues and the payment path; do not infer
  a universal voting threshold from isolated case commentary.

Registered capital and contribution:

- Check subscribed vs paid-in capital, contribution deadlines, acceleration clauses, founder contribution defects, and predecessor shareholder liability.
- Route current-law research through Company Law Articles 47, 51, 52, 54 and 88,
  the registered-capital transition regulation, and applicable temporal-effect
  authorities. Record the company formation date and equity-transfer date.
- Verify whether new investment is capital increase, equity transfer, or mixed primary/secondary sale.
- Check whether consideration, registered capital, capital reserve, and tax treatment are internally consistent.
- Do not equate capital reserve with paid-in registered capital. If an amount is
  converted or credited, verify accounting source, resolutions, statutory path,
  registration and cap-table treatment.
- For founders with unpaid registered capital, check whether the agreement requires payment within the Company Law statutory/transition period, a shorter negotiated period, or is silent.
- Check whether the articles and transaction documents address shareholder forfeiture after contribution default; most 2025 files did not, so absence should be flagged when relevant.
- For founder secondary sales, verify the transferred registered capital is fully paid or allocate unpaid-capital transfer liability expressly.

Articles vs shareholders agreement:

- Any special shareholder rights that need corporate-law effectiveness should be mirrored in the articles or resolutions where appropriate.
- Identify conflicts among investment agreement, shareholders agreement, articles, shareholder resolutions, and disclosure letter.
- For third-party enforceability or company governance issues, articles may matter more than pure contractual arrangements.

Governance:

- Reserved matters must distinguish shareholder-level and board-level approval.
- Avoid overbroad investor single-veto rights that create governance deadlock or merger-control/control concerns.
- Board appointment thresholds and observer rights should survive dilution only if the threshold remains met.
- For state-owned/government-backed or listed-company investors, confirm whether reserved matters, information rights, transfer restrictions, or exit rights need internal approval or disclosure-sensitive handling.
- If the transaction is an in-scope public state-asset transfer or capital
  increase, compare the agreement with the announcement/selection record and the
  2025 operation rules. Apply prohibited-term restrictions only after confirming scope.
- If insurance capital obtains control or joint control, check regulatory
  approval, own-funds/industry restrictions, prohibited financing support,
  control layers and look-through aggregation.

Anti-dilution:

- RMB deals cannot rely on preferred-share conversion mechanics in the same way as Cayman deals.
- Review whether adjustment is implemented through nominal-price capital increase, founder transfer, cash compensation, or other mechanism.
- Check tax, registration, capital maintenance, and enforceability.

Founder restrictions:

- Review non-compete/non-solicit period, scope, statutory employment limits, and reasonableness.
- For restricted equity, define good leaver/bad leaver, vested/unvested equity, price, buyer, and registration path.

Dispute resolution:

- PRC law is typical for RMB deals. For arbitration clauses used after
  2026-03-01, check the 2025 revised PRC Arbitration Law and current institution rules.
- Prefer clear arbitration clause, institution, seat, language, interim measures/preservation support, notice/service, consolidation where multiple documents exist.

## RMB Drafting Priorities by Side

Company/founder side:

- Narrow redemption triggers; add materiality and cure periods.
- Cap founder personal liability by equity/equity value or specific misconduct.
- Limit reserved matters and investor veto threshold.
- Add permitted transfers for founders and reasonable leaver mechanics.
- Keep information/inspection obligations practical and confidential.

Investor side:

- Preserve redemption collectability.
- Mirror key rights in articles/resolutions where needed.
- Require founder undertakings and enforceable remedies.
- Ensure anti-dilution, transfer rights, information rights, and liquidation economics are complete.
- Check that disclosure letter does not over-disclose away core warranties.
- For unpaid founder registered capital, require clear post-closing contribution covenants, default remedies, and articles consistency.
