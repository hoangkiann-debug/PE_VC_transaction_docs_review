# Party-Side Positions

Use these as defaults. Override when the user provides a playbook or deal-specific instruction.

## Company / Founder Side

Default objectives:

- Preserve operating flexibility and future financing flexibility.
- Avoid uncapped founder personal liability.
- Avoid automatic redemption for immaterial breaches.
- Avoid governance deadlocks and overly broad veto matters.
- Preserve permitted founder transfers and reasonable leaver treatment.
- Keep reporting, inspection, and consent obligations practical.

Preferred positions:

- Redemption: narrow triggers, cure periods, second-ranking founder obligation, equity/equity-value cap, no compounding above market benchmark.
- Anti-dilution: broad-based weighted average, carve-outs for ESOP, strategic issuances, M&A consideration, approved issuances.
- Liquidation preference: non-participating or capped participating; avoid stacking with high redemption return.
- Protective provisions: limited reserved matters; investor thresholds; no individual veto for small holders.
- Board: investor director only above threshold; observer confidentiality.
- Founder restrictions: permitted transfers; good/bad leaver distinctions; vested equity respected.
- Information rights: thresholds, confidentiality, frequency limits.

## Investor Side

Default objectives:

- Protect downside and exit path.
- Preserve influence over major corporate actions.
- Ensure collectability and enforceability.
- Prevent founder leakage and misalignment.
- Preserve future financing participation and anti-dilution protection.

Preferred positions:

- Redemption: QIPO, material breach, regulatory illegality, founder misconduct, cross-trigger; founder/company support depending on structure.
- Anti-dilution: at least weighted average; full ratchet only for strong investor posture or severe down-round concern.
- Liquidation preference: participating, seniority clear, no hidden cap unless negotiated.
- Protective provisions: core reserved matters with threshold appropriate to investor role.
- Board: director or observer rights, committees where needed.
- Information/inspection: periodic financials, budgets, annual audit, inspection rights.
- Transfer rights: affiliate/fund transfers, no overbroad competitor restriction, tag/ROFR sequencing clear.

## Lead Investor

Focus on governance, reserved matters, board rights, information, closing deliverables, and cross-investor coordination.

## Follow-On Investor

Focus on parity, MFN, side letters, no subordination to prior investors beyond accepted economics, and whether prior investor consent rights impair this round.

## Strategic Investor

Focus on competitor restrictions, information firewall, antitrust/control risk, business cooperation covenants, confidentiality, and transfer restrictions.

## Existing Shareholder / Prior-Round Investor

Focus on consent rights, seniority and inter-series ranking, anti-dilution impact,
waivers, MFN/side letters, release of prior claims, capitalization dilution, and
whether the new round silently amends or subordinates existing rights.

## State-Owned / Government-Backed Investor

Focus on internal authority, investment scope, valuation/filing requirements,
state-asset procedures, prohibited fixed-return or disguised-debt concerns,
exit approval, information/reporting obligations, and responsibility allocation
for approvals that the investor controls. Verify the applicable regulatory facts
rather than inferring them from the investor's name alone.

If the deal is publicly conducted through a property exchange, reconcile the
announcement, qualification/selection record and final agreement, and test the
2025 operation-rule restrictions on buyback, compensation, nominee holding,
disguised debt and price adjustment. Do not assume those public-process rules
govern every state-owned investor transaction.

## Insurance-Capital Investor

Determine whether the investor and affiliates obtain control or joint control
directly or through a look-through/aggregated structure. If the major-equity rule
applies, focus on regulatory approval as a true condition, own-funds and industry
eligibility, prohibited lending/guarantee support, cross-holding, control layers,
post-investment governance and anti-avoidance. Do not import those constraints
into an ordinary financial investment without confirming applicability.

## PRC Outbound Investor

Focus on funds certainty, ODI and foreign-exchange steps, information reporting,
outbound security review, export control, data, merger control, tax, state-asset
and sector approvals. Allocate reasonable efforts, filing control, information,
remedies, long-stop and termination consequences without requiring an unlawful
closing or unlimited regulatory-risk acceptance.

## Listed-Company Investor

Focus on disclosure timing, inside information/MNPI, related-party transaction
approval, investment mandate, consolidation/control implications, information
firewalls, and closing conditions tied to board/shareholder or exchange filings.

## Neutral / Deal-Facilitation Review

Use only when the user expressly requests a neutral drafting or consistency
review. Identify each material tradeoff and avoid silently selecting the company
or investor position. A neutral review does not remove the need to identify who
bears economic, control, execution, and enforcement risk.
