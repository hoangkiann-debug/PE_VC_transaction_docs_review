# Offshore USD Structure Playbook

Use when the offshore company, usually a Cayman company, is the financing subject. Subroute as direct holding or VIE.

## 2025 Market Signals

- Offshore structures were 33.49% of 2025 projects, rebounding from 2024 but still below older peaks; use the 2024/2025 average of 29.96% when a smoothed benchmark is needed.
- In 2025 offshore structures, direct holding remained the majority at 59.15%, while VIE structures rebounded to 40.85%.
- Hong Kong law was used in over 70% of 2025 offshore transaction documents; Delaware law appeared in 16.90%. Treat all non-PRC law conclusions as local-counsel confirmation points.
- Arbitration remained dominant across VC/PE projects, at 93.87% in 2025.

## Substructure

Direct holding:

- Offshore parent directly or indirectly holds PRC operating entities through equity.
- Review foreign investment restrictions, negative list, ODI/FDI, registration, tax, and exit path where PRC issues are relevant.
- For a PRC investor's outbound structure, apply the State Council regulation
  effective from 2026-07-01 and map the actual NDRC/MOFCOM/SAFE, security-review,
  export-control, data, tax and state-asset steps.

VIE:

- Offshore parent controls PRC operating entities through contractual control arrangements.
- Review control agreements, business licenses, regulatory sensitivity, PRC law change triggers, disclosure, and special rights termination for listing.

Mixed direct-holding/VIE:

- Treat the group as mixed when some PRC businesses are equity-held but regulated
  operations, licenses, data, IP, revenue or personnel remain in VIE entities.
- Build an entity-by-entity map instead of labeling the whole group simply
  "direct holding" or "VIE". Apply direct-holding checks to equity-held entities
  and VIE validity, control, license and unwinding checks to contract-controlled
  entities.
- A lower overall market share for offshore or VIE structures does not justify
  removing these checks. It changes the negotiation context, not the required
  legal and cross-document review for an actual offshore/VIE matter.

## Core Review Focus

Every key VC/PE clause in the benchmark remains relevant under offshore USD structure:

- SPA/SSA/IRA/SHA, M&A/MAA/M&AA/Articles, disclosure schedule, side letters, management rights, director indemnity, share restriction agreements.
- Preferred share rights, conversion price, anti-dilution, liquidation preference, dividends, redemption.
- Board composition, reserved matters, protective provisions, information/inspection/management rights.
- Founder vesting/restrictions, leaver repurchase, non-compete/non-solicit, founder secondary sale.
- ROFR, co-sale, drag, permitted transfers, competitor restrictions, MFN.
- Governing law, arbitration, service, interim measures, and jurisdiction-specific lawyer consultation.

## Offshore-Specific Checks

Articles/SHA consistency:

- Preferred share rights must be reflected consistently in Articles and SHA/IRA.
- Check share class names, conversion price, liquidation waterfall, voting thresholds, reserved matters, drag, ROFR/co-sale, transfer restrictions, redemption, and information rights.
- If Articles conflict with SHA, flag which should govern and which jurisdiction's practicing lawyer should be consulted.

SPA/SHA/Disclosure consistency:

- Check defined terms across SPA/SSA, SHA/IRA, Articles/M&A, and disclosure schedule.
- Confirm closing conditions and post-closing covenant survival.
- Check whether side letters create MFN, priority, information, or governance conflicts.
- Check whether partial-investor side agreements or settlements change redemption,
  waiver, release, amendment or dispute rights without the required parties or consent.

Direct holding PRC issues:

- Check whether PRC operating entities are foreign-invested enterprises and whether foreign investment restrictions apply.
- Identify PRC registration, tax, SAFE/ODI/FDI, and approvals as local-law confirmation points.
- Apply the 2025 SAFE cross-border investment/financing reform to current account
  and reinvestment steps, but separately retain Circular 37 analysis for relevant
  resident/SPV and round-trip facts.
- Because direct holding remained the majority of 2025 offshore projects, do not assume VIE documents exist merely because the financing subject is offshore.

VIE issues:

- Check that VIE documents are listed, executed, and consistent with control assumptions.
- Check triggers for redemption or termination if VIE contracts become invalid, unenforceable, or prohibited.
- Check listing/IPO termination of special shareholder rights and regulatory filing assumptions.
- Because 2025 VIE usage rebounded, keep VIE validity, control, license, PRC law-change, and unwinding/listing termination checks active whenever the operating business or regulated sector suggests VIE risk.

Non-PRC law:

- Do not provide final legal opinions on Cayman, Hong Kong, Delaware, New York, Singapore, or other non-PRC law.
- For company power, share redemption legality, enforceability of liquidation preference, fiduciary duties, director indemnity, governing law, and arbitration enforcement, use this notice where applicable: `涉及中国法律相关事项，请咨询执业中国律师；涉及非中国法管辖事项，请咨询相应法域的执业律师。`
- If a PRC-related or foreign-related arbitration clause may engage PRC
  arbitration law, separately check the institution, seat, interim-measure path,
  service, and enforcement interface under the law effective from 2026-03-01;
  do not collapse that analysis into the non-PRC governing-law conclusion.
- Compare dispute clauses across SPA/SSA, SHA/IRA, Articles, guarantees and side
  letters for joinder/consolidation, nonsignatories and parallel-proceeding risk.

Offshore ESOP:

- Reconcile authorized/reserved shares, pre/post-money pool, grant authority,
  vesting, leaver repurchase, change-of-control acceleration and Articles/cap-table treatment.
- Map PRC employee foreign-exchange, tax and service/IP arrangements to the
  actual award structure; do not assume one offshore plan works for every holder jurisdiction.

## Offshore Drafting Priorities by Side

Company/founder side:

- Align Articles/SHA and avoid overbroad vetoes.
- Limit drag triggers through valuation, timing, board/shareholder approval, and founder consent where appropriate.
- Keep redemption founder liability second-ranking or capped where possible.
- Preserve permitted transfers and founder estate/planning carve-outs.
- For offshore redemption, do not assume company-only obligor is still dominant; 2025 data shows company-only redemption obligor use declined and founder involvement increased.

Investor side:

- Ensure Articles hardwire preferred share rights.
- Preserve conversion, anti-dilution, liquidation, redemption, information, and veto rights.
- Add VIE/direct holding risk triggers if relevant.
- Require full package review, not only SPA/SHA.
